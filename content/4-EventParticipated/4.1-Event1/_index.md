# Reflection Report: "FCAJ Community Day (First Cloud Journey Community)"

### Purpose of the Event

- **Networking and Inspiration:** Create opportunities for members of the First Cloud Journey (FCJ) community to meet, exchange ideas, find potential collaborators, and build meaningful professional relationships.
- **Sharing Practical Knowledge:** Speakers discussed the AI-driven job market, effective AI usage (Prompt Engineering and AI Mindset), and practical AWS solutions such as Amazon CloudFront and Generative AI applications in enterprises.
- **Career Development Guidance:** Emphasized the importance of building strong technical foundations, developing real-world products instead of simple demos, and understanding business domains to remain competitive in today's job market.
- **Encouraging Action:** Motivated participants to stop procrastinating, continuously learn, and confidently apply for job opportunities.

### Speakers

- **Nguyễn Gia Hưng** – Solution Architect at AWS Vietnam and Founder of FCJ.
- **Tịnh Trương** – Platform Engineer at Gotam X.
- **Hải Anh** – Currently working at Pacific Vietnam.
- **Nguyễn Tuấn Thịnh** – DevOps Engineer.
- **Vy Lam** - Senior Business Systems Analyst, VPBank
### Key Highlights

#### Career Orientation in the AI Era

- The speakers emphasized the importance of developing an **AI mindset** and mastering AI tools such as **Prompt Engineering** to effectively apply AI in real-world scenarios.

#### Current Job Market

- Today's recruitment market requires candidates to possess not only soft skills and English proficiency but also strong technical expertise, the ability to build real-world products rather than simple demonstrations, and a university degree remains a significant advantage in competitive recruitment processes.

#### Solving Infrastructure Challenges on AWS

- **Cost Optimization:** The speakers analyzed the risks of traditional pay-as-you-go architectures, where unexpected traffic spikes can dramatically increase operational costs and create financial risks for businesses.
- **Advanced Security:** They highlighted the importance of implementing additional protection layers such as Amazon CloudFront, AWS WAF, and SYN Proxy to defend against DDoS and SYN Flood attacks more effectively than default security mechanisms.

#### Hackathon Experience

- The speakers shared practical experiences from hackathons, emphasizing the importance of solving real-world problems instead of focusing on overly ambitious ideas, while also highlighting teamwork as a key factor for success.

### What I Learned

#### Mindset in the AI Era

- **AI will not replace humans.** Instead, it makes software development faster, more efficient, and more affordable, resulting in increased demand for software solutions.
- **Context Engineering is essential.** Providing AI with clear context and objectives leads to much more accurate and useful results than asking vague or general questions.

#### Career Development

- **Avoid procrastination.** Technology evolves extremely quickly (AI capabilities are said to double approximately every four months), so it is important to start building and learning as soon as possible.
- **Technical knowledge alone is not enough.** Developers should also understand real-world business use cases, their role within a system, and how they contribute value to an organization.
- **A university degree still matters.** In today's highly competitive job market, it remains an important advantage during the recruitment screening process.

#### AWS Infrastructure Skills

- I learned how to optimize infrastructure costs and improve system security. Traditional architectures often lack effective cost estimation, which can lead to unexpected expenses. Therefore, implementing security services such as Amazon CloudFront, AWS WAF, and SYN Proxy is essential to protect systems against attacks like DDoS and SYN Flood.

### Lessons from the Hackathon (Teamwork)

- **Focus on practical solutions:** Instead of pursuing large-scale ideas, concentrate on solving real problems experienced by yourself or your users.
- **Leverage team strengths:** Assign tasks based on each member's strengths to improve productivity and deliver better results within limited time.

### Event Experience

- **An engaging networking atmosphere:** From the beginning of the event, participants were encouraged to introduce themselves to those sitting nearby, creating an open environment for making new friends and finding potential collaborators.
- **Practical insights from industry experts:** Rather than focusing only on theory, the speakers shared valuable real-world experiences about AI adoption, AWS infrastructure optimization (such as CloudFront and WAF), and enterprise project implementation.

#### Learning from Experienced Speakers

- Insights into AI and Prompt Engineering.
- AWS infrastructure problem-solving techniques.
- Practical lessons and teamwork experiences from hackathons.

#### Hands-on Technical Experience

- **Cost management:** Learn how to prevent unexpected billing spikes caused by traditional pay-as-you-go architectures.
- **Advanced security implementation:** Understand how Amazon CloudFront, AWS WAF, and SYN Proxy work together to provide stronger protection against DDoS and SYN Flood attacks.
- **Context Engineering:** Learn how to communicate effectively with AI by providing sufficient context, leading to more accurate and relevant outputs.
- **Solution-oriented development (Core Features):** Rather than adding unnecessary large-scale features, the team focused on solving real user pain points, resulting in a more complete and practical product.

#### Key Takeaways

- **Practice Context Engineering** to help AI better understand specific requirements and objectives, resulting in more accurate responses.
- **Take action immediately.** Since technology evolves rapidly, delaying learning or building projects can cause you to fall behind.
- **Develop business knowledge in addition to technical skills.** Understanding enterprise use cases and business requirements is becoming increasingly important.
- **Focus on solving practical problems** instead of pursuing overly ambitious ideas.
- **Value teamwork.** Effective collaboration and task distribution significantly improve project outcomes.
